The Car

Car Body

Our body is modelled after modern 4x4 cars in production today. Designed by our team member Jeff, we started with a 2D face of the car then extruded it backwards to create the depth. This created a framework for which the cars body was modelled around. The main material used in the body is recycled paper from our school. This keeps the body extremely lightweight and performant while remaining sustainable. The top finish is a paper weave spray painted black, to give a matte black effect. Our team logo, and sponsor's were also applied to the body for the final competition.

Looking forward to the national finals we have started a comprehensive redesign of the body, opting for a less "boxy" shape, utilising more sleek lines and curves. Watch this space for our final design! If you are knowledgeable and would like to get in touch, don't hesitate to contact us.

Chassis

Our Chassis was based on a symmetrical trapezium to enable a 4-wheel steering system. We decided to use lightweight plywood sustainably sourced and then laser cut into the needed shape. We etched our team logo into the side and sprayed it black to keep with the overall theme.
Our chassis is seperated from the body and holds the internals of our electronics.

For the nationals finals we are re-thinking our approach to give us the capacity for our new ideas. If you would like to work with us on our body don't hesitate to get in contact.

Electronics

Our car utilises a Light Dependent Resistor and Tilt Sensor system to fulfill the competition regulations. For our national finals we want to step-up the complexity and functionality of the car. We have exciting ideas for innovations which we would like to implement. If you would like to support us please don't hesitate to get in contact.
